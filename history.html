Brief History

lest's see how it all began

  • 1

    1967

    The first Super Bowl halftime show featured university marching bands from the University of Arizona and the University of Michigan. The bands performed traditional marches and patriotic songs.

  • 2

    1976

    The halftime show presented a "Salute to America's Bicentennial," featuring performances by Up with People, a musical group known for their positive message and energetic performances.

  • 3

    1993

    Michael Jackson's halftime performance in Super Bowl XXVII marked a turning point in the production and scale of halftime shows. Jackson's performance was a grand spectacle with elaborate set designs, choreography, and special effects. It set a new standard for future halftime shows, inspiring the use of larger stages, high-profile performers, and the integration of technology to create more visually stunning and entertaining performances.

  • 4

    2001

    The halftime show showcased a collaboration between popular music acts *NSYNC, Britney Spears, Aerosmith, and rapper Nelly. This performance highlighted the trend of featuring a mix of top artists from different genres to appeal to a wide audience.

  • 5

    2004

    The halftime show of Super Bowl XXXVIII, featuring Janet Jackson and Justin Timberlake, had a profound impact on the way halftime shows were produced and regulated. The "wardrobe malfunction" incident, where Janet Jackson's breast was briefly exposed, sparked a nationwide controversy and led to increased scrutiny and regulations regarding content and censorship of halftime performances. This incident resulted in stricter pre-show rehearsals, delay systems to avoid live broadcasting mishaps, and a more cautious approach to ensure family-friendly entertainment.
